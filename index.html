A day at Kids Camp

From sunrise to lights out — what a day at Allaso looks like.

Here's a look at what a typical day looks like for a 2nd – 5th grader at Allaso Ranch — from the moment they wake up to the moment their head hits the pillow.

Note: the schedule varies on Sunday (arrival) and Friday (departure) day.

8:30 AM
Breakfast
Family-style in the dining hall. When the staff wheels out the food, "Eye of the Tiger" blasts and campers stand on chairs to dance. Your kid will come home doing it.
9:00 AM
Devotional Time
Cabin gathers to connect with God, read Scripture, and pray. A quieter moment before the day picks up speed.
9:30 AM
Challenge Time
Color teams compete — relay races, group games, friendly (sometimes intense) competition. Builds camaraderie fast.
10:15 AM
Activities · Rotations 1 & 2
Two one-hour blocks. By the end of the week, every camper has tried everything — and been to the water park at least twice a week.
12:30 PM
Lunch
Back to the dining hall. More family-style with cabin mates. And yes — more "Eye of the Tiger."
1:00 PM
Bible Study
Trainers go deeper with their cabin — discussing what they're learning, answering questions, applying it to real life.
1:45 PM
Back on Bunk
A short rest period. Campers recharge in their cabins. (Trainers appreciate this time too.)
2:30 PM
Activities · Rotations 3, 4 & 5
Three more rotations through 5:45 PM. By dinner, kids are sunscreened, sweaty, and smiling. Clean-up follows.
6:30 PM
Dinner
By now, your camper knows the songs, the cheers, and is standing on their chair before the music even starts.
7:00 PM
Dessert on Deck
Dessert on the Grand Deck overlooking Allaso Ranch Lake. A sweet pause — literally — before the evening program.
7:30 PM
Camp at Night
The heartbeat of Kids Camp. Worship designed for kids, fun games, and an engaging Bible lesson. Where many campers meet Jesus for the first time.
8:30 PM
Evening Activities
Cabin hangouts, swim party, group games, or low-key time before bed.
9:30 PM
Reflections / Lights Out
The cabin processes the day together, prays, and settles in. (Okay — sometimes there's whispering. But eventually, sleep wins.)

The activities they'll rotate through

Zip Lines — Soar 60 feet above the ground

Giant Swing — Not for the faint of heart

Wibit — Obstacle course on Allaso Lake

Equestrian Center — Saddle up for horseback riding

Archery Tag — Ready, aim, fire

Swim Center — Lazy river, water slides, splash pool, water volleyball

Canoes & Kayaks — Explore Allaso Ranch Lake

Sports Fields — Basketball, kickball, soccer, pickleball, and more

Throughout the week, the teaching builds toward an invitation — an opportunity for kids to begin a relationship with Jesus. On Thursday, there's a special Baptism Class for kids who've made that decision.

From the Allaso Team

If this is the first time you've sent your child, you have questions.

We get it. Sending your child to camp — maybe for the first time — is a big deal. So here's exactly what we do, every day, to make sure your kid is safe, seen, and known while they're with us.

18+ Years of Camp
10k+ Campers Served
5k+ Lives Changed
Our Leaders
Each volunteer and staff member undergoes a thorough interview process, a background check which includes a check against the national sex offender registry, and reference checks at the time of hire.
Daily Oversight
Your child is never alone. From wake-up to lights-out, campers are with a trained adult at all times — and daily operations are monitored by Ministry Team Leaders, Activity Managers, the Waterfront Director, and the Camp Director.
Abuse Prevention
We partner with Ministry Safe, an outside organization that trains our staff and helps us identify and eliminate risk areas across the ranch. It's the same standard the country's most-trusted camps use.
On the Water
American Red Cross-certified lifeguards at every water activity. Life jackets required for non-swimmers, every time. Pool water tested regularly to Texas state standards.
On-Site Medical
Our Medical Center is staffed by licensed health professionals and overseen by a physician who reviews our protocols every year. Closest ER is 25 minutes away; EMTs are closer.
Emergencies
We have written, practiced procedures for severe weather, fire, medical events, intruders, and missing campers — and every staff member is trained on their role before camp week begins.
Stay Connected
Send messages we'll print and deliver. Browse camp photos and get alerted when your kid shows up in one.

The honest answer

What you get for $999 — and how to know if your kid is ready.

A few weeks out, every parent runs the same two math problems: is this worth it, and is my kid ready. Here are honest answers to both.

The cost is $999 per camper, all-in — lodging, meals, every activity, the t-shirt. *Payment plans are available.

The cost — and how it compares

Allaso Ranch · Kids Camp $999
Pine Cove $1,699
Sky Ranch $1,784
Published 2026 rates for a comparable elementary-age overnight week.

What's included

5 nights of cabin lodging on the ranch
All meals — breakfast, lunch, dinner, snacks
Every activity: Zip Lines, Giant Swing, Wibit, Equestrian Center, Archery Tag, Swim Center, Canoes & Kayaks, Sports Fields
Daily worship & small-group teaching
Kids Camp 2026 t-shirt & water bottle

What your child will come home with

A newfound independence and confidence
Stories about zip lines, horses, and late-night cabin conversations
New friends (and a request to sign up again next year)
A Christ-centered foundation that will stick with them

Is my kid ready?

Most kids are more ready than their parents think. Here's the honest read:

Probably yes if your camper has done a sleepover, can shower without a parent, and gets excited (even if a little nervous) when you describe camp.

A few tips that help. Talk it through together. Pack the bag together so they know where everything is. Tuck a letter from home into their bag.

If homesickness hits — and for some kids, it will — our leaders are trained for it. They sit with the camper, they call us, we call you. It usually passes by day two.
